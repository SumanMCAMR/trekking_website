Leh: Visit the monasteries of Shey, Thikse and Hemis

Morning departure by private car with guide and visit of the Royal Fortress of Shey, built on the crest of a mountain, the monastery of Shey was the first castle of the king of Ladakh, Lachen Palgyigon and his successor Dhamarajas. Then visit the monastery of Thikse, which overlooks the valley and has a very large statue of the Buddha, afternoon visit of the monasteries of Hemis built in 1605 by Stagsang Raschen, they group together more than 500 monks, They shelter a unique collection of small paintings retracing the life of the great masters of Lamaism. Visits: Stok Palace, Spituk Monastery and Choklamsar camp

Visit by private car with guide, of the valley: from the Royal Palace of Stok official residence of the former royal family of Ladakh, its small museum with beautiful collections of jewels and thankas. The monastery was built by King Tsespal Tondup Namgyal in 1825. Afternoon visit Spituk Monastery, located 8km from Leh on a small hill. The monastery was built in the 11th century by 'Od-de'. Huge statues of Goddess Kali with her face always covered is to be seen in the monastery temple. At the end of the day, visit the Tibetan refugee camp and school of Choklamsar which has become an important center for the study of Tibetan literature and history. Night at the hotel.

Leh, Alchi and Lamayuru

Morning after breakfast, departure by Jeep for Alchi Monastery. Located 69kms west of Leh. The sacred enclosure "of Alchi consists of 6 temples, erected in the plain, the oldest of which date from the 11th century, painted walls and sculptures created by ancient artists from Tibet, Kashmir and Afghanistan, they testify to the influence of Kashmiri art at that time they are among the most interesting in Ladakh by their architecture (carved wooden porches in front of the temples) and the representation of statues and frescoes Following the visit, departure in jeep for Lamayuru. Located 126kms from Leh. Behind the village on the hill. Discovery of the Yung-Drung monastery of Lamayuru founded in the 11th century. . It is famous for its statues of the Buddha, its rich wood reliefs and the only examples of Kashmiri wall paintings existing in the region. The village contains many chortens. Night at the hotel.

Lamayuru- Rizong - Likir and back to Leh

Depart Lamayuru for Rizong Monastery. A rocky fortress perched on the mountainside in a grandiose setting. This monastery is also known as Yuma Changchublin Monastery, and was built around 135 years ago by Lama Tsultim Nima. Following the visit, departure to visit the monastery of Likir. This monastery belongs to the Galupa and was built in 1065 by Lachen Gyalpo, 5th king of Ladakh. Return to Leh. Night at the hotel.
