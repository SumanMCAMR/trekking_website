Singalila > Dhor (3750m)

After breakfast we start with a steep climb through rhododendron forests, along the way you will see the prayer flags. Then we descend to an altitude of 3300 meters, the path between Nepal and Sikkim, offering spectacular views. We continue to follow the ridge. The rhododendron forests in spring are in full bloom, adding color to this already stunning walk. We stop for lunch before continuing to the other ridge and then to the yak herders camp at Dhor (3720m). We will camp on the Nepal side of the ridge and offer splendid views, as well as a beautiful sunset panorama of the high peaks of Nepal, such as Makalu and Everest. 7 to 8 hours of walking.

Dhor > Paharay Megu > (3940m)

We get up early to watch the sunrise over Kanchenjunga - the 3rd highest mountain in the world. After breakfast we start the trek. Most of the day is up and down and in the prayer flags. The elevation is between 3600m and 3900m, camping at Paharay Megu (3930m), which is another grazing ground for yaks. 5 to 6 hours of walking.


5-6 hour Trekking

3940m

Paharay Megu > Lamapokhari (4200m)

After breakfast the hike begins, the terrain more open and the view of the mountains larger. We follow a wide valley passing a large waterfall, our campsite near the sacred lake at Lampokhari (4200m). We would reach lunch camp, but we would have a long day tomorrow. There are several sacred lakes around our campsite and some are very important for pilgrims from Sikkim who come here during the monsoon months. In the evening we have a beautiful view by a sunset over Makalu and other high peaks. 3 to 4 hours of walking.

Panding > Dzongri (4130m)

Morning we descend to the Bikbari river, about 3 hours of trekking. Going down the rhododendron forest. After crossing the river, we gently climb to a small pass from where you reach the vast Chaurikhang (4120m) and Dzongri grasslands. 4 to 5 hours walking.


4-5 hour walking

4130m

Dzongri > Lamune (4165m)

Beginning of the morning trek half an hour walk, the spectacular view of the sunrise over Kangchenjunga and the other neighboring peaks of Rathong (6683m.), Kokthang (6150m.), Kabru Dome (6604m.) and Forked Peak (6116m.)..... Today's walk begins with a short climb to a ridge, which offers a clear view of Sikkim. We follow this scenic ridge for about 5 km, before descending to our very steep lunch spot, on the shores of the icy Praig Chu. Then we cross the river to start the climb to Thangsing, a large open campsite on a pasture. The southern ridge of Kangchenjunga and the moraines of the Oglathang glacier are visible just ahead of you, there are close views of Pandim. We had lunch at Thangsing and continue another 2 hours camping at Lamune (4164m). VS' is an easy walk in a wide valley after crossing the stream with Kanchenjunga. 6 to 7 hours of walking

Night In Tent

Lamune > Goecha la > Samity Lake

After breakfast we start the trek for next destination. We will follow a moraine to Samiti Pokhari. The holy lake surrounded by prayer flags and number of snow capped peaks including Pandim which is at a touching distance. From there we will do the difficult trek, about 4 hours uphill to 4800 meters at Goecha La. The trail to the lake. Before climbing a series of morainic ridges on the eastern border of the Oglathang Glacier. More than 4000 meters above the Talung Glacier, at a distance of only 5 km from our viewpoint, is the eastern wall of Kangchenjunga, which includes the five main peaks of the massif and also the very impressive Kabru and Rathong peaks. We go back to Samity lake at the start of the afternoon and lunch before continuing the descent for an hour at the same Lamune campsite, or continue to Thangsing. 9 to 10 hours from the market.


4 hour walking

Samity Lake > Tsokha (3500m)

Very easy hike today. We start the trek to the river where we take another path, pass Dzongri and Phedang, cross the forest and descend through thick, tall rhododendrons and magnolias, then we come to the small village of Tsokha, not many locals. 5 to 6 hours from the market.


5-6 hour walking

3500m

Tsokha > Yuksom

Continue downhill through rhododendron forests to the suspension bridge over the Praigchu, from where you follow the track up and down and again through the forests to the large village of Yuksom. Here we spend the night in a hotel. Yuksom is an important place in the history of Sikkim as the first King of Sikkim was crowned here and his seat can still be seen. You can take a walk to Dubdi Monastery - the first monastery in Sikkim - best time to visit in the evening or early morning. 5 to 6 hours from the market
